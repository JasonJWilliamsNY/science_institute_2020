Quiz Review Sheet

Quiz: Lab math and mitochondrial DNA introduction

Ground rules/instructions

  1. No talking - During the quiz there is absolutely no talking. If you have a question, please raise your hand. If I can fairly answer your question (for example about a ambiguous statement or error) I will and will inform the other quiz takers.
  2. Spread out - you must have an empty chair to your right and left.
  3. You must bring your Chromebook - quiz will be online
  4. When the music stops you must submit your quiz - more info on this in class.

Review

Review the lab math "Binder" computer activity, as well as our notes on lab math. Also review our notes on mitochondrial DNA and the "Mitochondrial DNA Basics" slides 1-20. There will not be any questions about DNA Sequencing or PCR.

For math/mole problems you will be provided a periodic table. You may also use a calculator.

  • Be able to define a mole
  • Be able to describe the relationship between a mole and an element's molecular weight
  • Know how to calculate the molecular (formula) weight for a molecule when given the number and type of atomic components (e.g. What is the formula weight for C6H12O6?)
  • Know what a stock solution is, and why we make stock solutions in the lab
  • Know the C1V1 = C2V2 equation and be able to solve it in order to tell how much of a stock solution is needed to make a required quantity of a less concentrated solution
  • Know how to calculate the molarity of a solution (number of moles of solute per liter solvent)
  • Know at least two key differences between mitochondrial and nuclear DNA
  • Know what section of mitochondrial DNA we are examining in our laboratory and why we have chosen this section of DNA (i.e. it is not a section of DNA that encodes protein, it will not reveal any potentially sensitive medical information)
